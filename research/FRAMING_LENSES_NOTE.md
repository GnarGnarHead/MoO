# Framing Lenses Note

> Status: peripheral research framing.
>
> This note keeps outside mathematical research in its proper place. Euler,
> Fourier, Hodge, Ramanujan, Hardy, Stern-Brocot, Calkin-Wilf, continued
> fractions, integer complexity, experimental mathematics, and related bodies
> of work are not part of MoO's core grammar. They are framings used to ask
> better questions after MoO has recorded witnessed emergence.

## Boundary

MoO's core is not imported from outside mathematics.

The core remains:

```text
1
fundamental operator fan
orders
branches
witnessed emergence
field of observation
projected form
```

External research does not define any of these terms.

External research also does not validate MoO by association. A famous name,
classical theorem, or standard method is not MoO evidence. The evidence is
still the witnessed emergence itself.

## Allowed Role

Outside research is allowed as a framing lens when it helps ask:

```text
What branch or relation should be inspected?
What ordinary explanation might account for this pattern?
What false reading should be guarded against?
What projection might reveal or obscure witnessed emergence?
What control or comparison would make the observation less fragile?
```

This means a framing lens may suggest a question, a comparison, or a possible
projection. It may not become the answer.

## Not Core

Do not put outside research into the root claim stack as if it is part of MoO's
origin.

Bad reading:

```text
Euler explains MoO.
Fourier/Hodge defines MoO edge structure.
Stern-Brocot is the MoO rational field.
Ramanujan-style projection licenses MoO constants.
Hardy-Littlewood style arcs define MoO circles or primes.
```

Better reading:

```text
Euler helps frame reciprocal-mass, product, zero, and pi-shadow questions.
Fourier/Hodge helps frame projection and edge-flow questions.
Stern-Brocot/Calkin-Wilf/Farey help frame rational-baseline questions.
Integer complexity helps frame witnessed-cost questions.
Ramanujan/Hardy-Littlewood help frame later asymptotic and projection questions.
Experimental mathematics helps frame discipline, controls, and kill rules.
```

## Current Use By Family

### Euler

Euler is useful because much of his work reads constants through exact
structures: sums, products, zeros, reciprocal powers, and factorization.

MoO use:

```text
frame exact finite reciprocal-square, product, return, and pi-shadow questions
```

MoO non-use:

```text
do not claim MoO constructs pi because Euler relates pi to reciprocal squares
```

### Fourier / Hodge / Spectral Methods

These are useful because MoO is not only a value list. It records witnessed
relations, and some relations can collapse under a value projection while
remaining meaningful as edge structure.

MoO use:

```text
frame questions about projection, cancellation, return, preservation, and
edge-flow structure
```

MoO non-use:

```text
do not replace witnessed emergence with spectral summaries
```

### Rational Enumeration Baselines

Stern-Brocot, Calkin-Wilf, Farey order, continued fractions, and related
rational systems are useful baselines for the positive-spine rational shadow.

MoO use:

```text
ask whether a MoO ordering, witness pattern, or branch visibility is explained
by ordinary rational height, denominator, tree depth, or continued-fraction
structure
```

MoO non-use:

```text
do not identify MoO itself with a rational enumeration tree
```

### Integer Complexity And Construction Cost

Integer complexity, addition chains, straight-line programs, and circuit-style
costs are useful because MoO cares how a value emerges, not merely whether it
appears.

MoO use:

```text
frame witnessed-cost comparisons across branches and fields of observation
```

MoO non-use:

```text
do not reduce MoO witness to a standard shortest-expression score
```

### Ramanujan / Hardy-Littlewood / Asymptotic Work

This material is useful only after exact MoO structure exists. It can help
frame projected forms, asymptotic shadows, and later organization tests.

MoO use:

```text
frame careful Order-4 projection questions from named source families
```

MoO non-use:

```text
do not use asymptotic prestige to promote a weak pattern
```

### Experimental Mathematics

Experimental mathematics is useful as discipline: controls, holdouts,
counterexamples, exact reports, and kill rules.

MoO use:

```text
protect the project from mistaking excitement for evidence
```

MoO non-use:

```text
do not turn MoO into target-hunting for impressive constants or famous
patterns
```

## Interpretation Rule

When a research note invokes an outside body of work, translate it back into
MoO language before treating it as meaningful:

```text
outside framing
-> MoO question
-> witnessed emergence or absent witness
-> branch / order / field reading
-> possible projected form only after repeated structure
```

If the chain cannot be completed, the outside research remains a framing note,
not a MoO result.

## Governing Sentence

```text
External research frames questions around MoO; it does not enter the core,
replace witnesses, or promote projected forms by authority.
```
